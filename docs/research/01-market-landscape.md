# FMIQ Market Landscape — IWMS / CMMS Competitive Study

Version: v0.1 | Generated: 2026-06-11 | Owner: Nexum Intelligence Systems Limited

**Scope.** Competitive market study for FMIQ — an IWMS targeting Irish/UK public-sector, heritage, and cultural estates, with the National Museum of Ireland (NMI) Preliminary Market Consultation as the anchor requirements source. Covers: (1) enterprise IWMS incumbents, (2) modern mobile-first CMMS challengers, (3) heritage/museum-specific FM tooling, (4) public-sector buying patterns in Ireland and the UK, (5) FMIQ differentiation thesis.

**Method note.** Compiled from vendor sites, G-Cloud/Find a Tender/eTenders notices, analyst material (Gartner Peer Insights, Verdantix), and review aggregators (G2, Capterra, SoftwareAdvice, SelectHub), June 2026. Pricing signals are directional, not quotes. The NMI PMC itself (project instructions) is treated as the authoritative requirements statement.

---

## 1. Market context

The global IWMS market is estimated at roughly USD 6–7 billion in 2026, growing at 12–15% CAGR depending on source ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/integrated-workplace-management-system-market), [KBV Research](https://www.kbvresearch.com/integrated-workplace-management-system-market/), [MarketsandMarkets](https://www.marketsandmarkets.com/Market-Reports/integrated-workplace-management-system-market-49902186.html)). Two structural shifts matter for FMIQ:

1. **The enterprise tier is consolidating and repositioning.** IBM has folded TRIRIGA into the Maximo Application Suite ([IBM](https://www.ibm.com/products/tririga)); Archibus and Serraview merged under Eptura ([Eptura history](https://eptura.com/resource/eptura-history/)); MRI acquired FSI/Concept Evolution ([MRI news](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/news/fsi-becomes-mri-software/)); Verdantix frames the top of the market as moving from IWMS to "Connected Portfolio Intelligence Platforms" ([Verdantix](https://www.verdantix.com/venture/report/market-insight-the-transformation-of-iwms-to-connected-portfolio-intelligence-platforms-cpip)). The incumbents are getting broader, heavier, and more expensive — not easier to deploy.
2. **The usability tier is growing fastest.** MaintainX raised a $150M Series D at a $2.5B valuation in July 2025, with ~$115M ARR and 11,000+ customers ([MaintainX newsroom](https://www.getmaintainx.com/newsroom/maintainx-raises-150m), [Getlatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/getmaintainx.com)). It tops G2's CMMS/EAM rankings on ease of use (9.4/10) ([G2 Winter 2026](https://www.getmaintainx.com/blog/maintainx-g2-winter-2026-reports)). The mobile-first CMMS class has proven that frontline adoption — not module breadth — is what determines whether an FM system is actually used.

The gap in the middle: nobody offers enterprise-IWMS governance (compliance, audit, statutory maintenance) with consumer-grade onboarding, and nobody at all combines that with collection-care environmental intelligence for heritage estates.

## 2. Enterprise IWMS incumbents

### 2.1 Planon

- **Positioning.** Market-leading "true IWMS"/CPIP for large enterprises: real estate, space, maintenance, sustainability, IoT on one platform ([Planon](https://planonsoftware.com/us/), [PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/planon-group-strengthens-market-leading-position-in-worldwide-iwms-market-577911521.html)). Gartner names it the largest vendor with IWMS as its primary revenue stream ([Gartner Market Guide](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/3887777)).
- **Target segment.** Large corporates, healthcare systems, universities, public bodies. Explicitly not a lightweight CMMS; no SMB tier, no self-serve ([Facilio review](https://facilio.com/blog/planon-review/)).
- **Public-sector/heritage presence.** Listed on UK G-Cloud as IWMS/CAFM/CPIP for public sector ([Digital Marketplace](https://www.applytosupply.digitalmarketplace.service.gov.uk/g-cloud/services/734741321099316)); named among the seven NHS CAFM providers ([MRI blog, NHS CAFM providers 2026](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/blog/nhs-cafm-providers-uk-2026/)). No visible Irish museum or OPW flagship reference.
- **Pricing signals.** Quote-based enterprise pricing reported from ~$200,000/year; certified-partner implementations of 9–18 months ([Facilio](https://facilio.com/blog/planon-review/), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/70410/Planon/)).
- **Strengths.** Breadth and stability; sustainability/IoT depth; 84% aggregate user satisfaction ([SelectHub](https://www.selecthub.com/p/iwms-software/planon/)).
- **Weaknesses (reviews).** Implementation complexity ("navigating a labyrinth"), steep learning curve, slow UI modernisation, enterprise-only pricing ([SelectHub](https://www.selecthub.com/p/iwms-software/planon/), [SoftwareAdvice](https://www.softwareadvice.com/cafm/planon-universe-profile/)).

### 2.2 IBM TRIRIGA / Maximo Real Estate and Facilities

- **Positioning.** Enterprise IWMS for real estate, capital projects, space, and facilities; now repackaged as "Maximo Real Estate and Facilities" inside Maximo Application Suite, with AI/IoT analytics emphasis ([IBM](https://www.ibm.com/products/tririga), [FitGap](https://us.fitgap.com/products/011959/ibm-maximo-real-estate-and-facilities-formerly-ibm-tririga)).
- **Target segment.** Mid-to-large enterprise: manufacturing, healthcare, higher education, government ([Technology Evaluation](https://www3.technologyevaluation.com/solutions/53700/ibm-tririga)).
- **Public-sector presence.** On UK G-Cloud (SaaS and on-prem; 30-day on-prem trials offered) ([Digital Marketplace](https://www.applytosupply.digitalmarketplace.service.gov.uk/g-cloud/services/966207094456536)); listed among NHS CAFM providers ([MRI blog](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/blog/nhs-cafm-providers-uk-2026/)). Heavy in central government estates globally; no Irish cultural-institution visibility.
- **Pricing signals.** User-based licensing; entry reported around $42,000/year before implementation, add-ons (Maximo integration, Smart Building Insights) extra; TCO dominated by implementation/customisation ([SaaSworthy](https://www.saasworthy.com/product/ibm-tririga/pricing), [SoftwareAdvice UK](https://www.softwareadvice.co.uk/software/395538/ibm-tririga)).
- **Strengths.** Capital projects + lease accounting depth; FedRAMP-class security posture; IBM ecosystem.
- **Weaknesses.** The MAS migration creates churn risk in its install base; cost and complexity at the extreme end; product identity in flux (TRIRIGA → MAS) ([Banetti](https://info.banetti.com/tririga-vs-maximo/)). For a museum-scale estate this is an oil tanker procured to do a harbour ferry's job.

### 2.3 Eptura (Archibus)

- **Positioning.** Archibus is the long-established IWMS (space, assets, maintenance, capital), now one product line inside Eptura alongside Condeco/iOffice workplace tools ([Eptura](https://eptura.com/our-platform/archibus/), [Eptura history](https://eptura.com/resource/eptura-history/)).
- **Target segment.** Large organisations with complex estates: corporates, healthcare, higher education, government. FedRAMP-authorised "Archibus for Government" for US federal ([TrustRadius](https://www.trustradius.com/products/archibus/reviews)).
- **Public-sector/heritage presence.** Strong in universities and government via partners (JLL implements/manages it — [JLL](https://www.jll.com/en-us/products/managed-technology/archibus)). No documented Irish/UK museum flagship found in this research pass.
- **Pricing signals.** Quote-based, module-licensed, partner-implemented; analyst profiles place it firmly in the enterprise budget class ([Technology Evaluation](https://www3.technologyevaluation.com/solutions/54115/archibus)).
- **Strengths.** Flexibility, powerful reporting, very deep module catalogue; strong CAD/BIM/space heritage ([TrustRadius](https://www.trustradius.com/products/archibus/reviews)).
- **Weaknesses (reviews).** Steep learning curve; Web Central UI "too complex, created for power users"; many steps to reach features ([G2](https://www.g2.com/products/archibus/reviews), [TrustRadius](https://www.trustradius.com/products/archibus/reviews)).

### 2.4 MRI Evolution (formerly FSI Concept Evolution)

- **Positioning.** UK-grown enterprise CAFM/IWMS; hard + soft FM, contractor management, compliance, IoT, now under MRI Software after the 2021 FSI acquisition ([MRI](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/products/evolution/), [MRI news](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/news/fsi-becomes-mri-software/)).
- **Target segment.** UK FM service providers and estate owners; explicit NHS play "aligned to the Government's 10 Year Health Plan" ([MRI healthcare](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/healthcare-facilities-management-software/)).
- **Public-sector presence.** The most credible UK public-estates incumbent of the group: Guy's & St Thomas' (Essentia) is implementing it; on G-Cloud ([Digital Marketplace](https://www.applytosupply.digitalmarketplace.service.gov.uk/g-cloud/services/507168932461863)); MRI itself publishes the NHS CAFM provider landscape ([MRI blog](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/blog/nhs-cafm-providers-uk-2026/)). This is the likeliest "proven, already live" bidder profile for an NMI-type tender.
- **Pricing signals.** Quote-based; Capterra UK lists it without published tiers ([Capterra UK](https://www.capterra.co.uk/software/32352/concept)). Mid-enterprise: cheaper than Planon/TRIRIGA, well above CMMS per-seat rates.
- **Strengths.** Hard-FM depth (PPM, SFG20-style regimes, contractor/SLA management); UK compliance fluency; healthcare references.
- **Weaknesses.** Legacy Concept lineage shows in UX; configuration-heavy implementations; roll-up ownership (MRI is PE-backed and acquisitive) means roadmap split across many products ([FMJ](https://www.fmj.co.uk/software-evolution/)).

### 2.5 Service Works Global (QFM)

- **Positioning.** Mid-enterprise CAFM + BIM + PPP performance management; UK HQ with Sweden/Australia/Canada operations ([SWG](https://www.swg.com/)).
- **Target segment.** Healthcare, education, defence, government estates, and PPP/PFI operators — SWG manages software for 200+ PPP contracts ([SWG public sector](https://www.swg.com/can/cafm-software-public-sector/)).
- **Public-sector/heritage presence.** NSW Parliament House, universities (Curtin), justice/health PPPs ([SWG case studies](http://www.swg.com/about-us/case-studies/)); on G-Cloud and markets that route explicitly ([SWG on G-Cloud](https://www.swg.com/news/using-g-cloud-for-easier-cafm-procurement/)). Museum-sector references not surfaced.
- **Pricing signals.** Quote-based; mid-market CAFM band. Gartner Peer Insights reviews exist but volumes are thin ([Gartner Peer Insights](https://www.gartner.com/reviews/market/integrated-workplace-management-systems/vendor/service-works-global/product/qfm)).
- **Strengths.** PPP/contract-performance niche; solid helpdesk + planned maintenance core.
- **Weaknesses.** Smaller R&D base than MRI/Planon; dated UI by modern CMMS standards; limited brand pull outside its niches.

### 2.6 Civica

- **Positioning.** GovTech generalist; Civica Property Management is its estates/CAFM suite for public bodies — asset lifecycle, statutory compliance (fire, asbestos, legionella), capital works, carbon tracking ([Civica](https://www.civica.com/en-gb/product-pages/property-management-software/), [product overview PDF](https://www.civica.com/globalassets/7.document-downloads/2.uk-docs/product-information/property-management/civica-property-management-product-overview.pdf)).
- **Target segment.** UK/Irish local and central government, NHS, fire, police; trusted by 150+ public bodies including Homes England ([Truth About Local Government review](https://www.truthaboutlocalgovernment.com/post/the-top-property-management-systems-for-uk-local-government-in-2025-a-comparative-review)).
- **Public-sector presence in Ireland.** Civica has substantial Irish government business across its wider portfolio, making it a politically comfortable choice — but property/CAFM is one product line among hundreds, not its centre of gravity.
- **Pricing signals.** Quote-based, public-sector framework pricing.
- **Strengths.** Compliance module with automated statutory-inspection alerts; finance/HR/GIS integration; incumbent trust in government ([Civica local government](https://www.civica.com/en-gb/sector-pages/local-government/)).
- **Weaknesses.** Product is corporate-estate oriented; not heritage- or collection-aware; innovation cadence of a large GovTech conglomerate; FM is a side business.

## 3. Modern mobile-first CMMS challengers

### 3.1 Why they win

The challenger class (MaintainX, UpKeep, Limble, Fiix, Fracttal) wins on a single axis the incumbents structurally cannot match: **time-to-adoption**. Common pattern:

- Mobile-first work orders with photo/voice capture; technicians onboard in hours, not training courses.
- Self-serve trials and published per-seat pricing — procurement can evaluate without a sales cycle.
- Deployment in days-to-weeks versus the 9–18 months of enterprise IWMS ([Facilio on Planon](https://facilio.com/blog/planon-review/) vs [Limble comparison](https://limble.com/learn/cmms/software-comparison/)).
- G2-validated usability as the marketing engine: MaintainX ease-of-use 9.4/10, #1 in CMMS and EAM, perfect satisfaction score ([MaintainX/G2](https://www.getmaintainx.com/blog/maintainx-tops-g2-fall-2025-cmms-and-eam-rankings)).

### 3.2 Vendor notes

| Vendor | Pricing signal | What enterprise buyers say is missing |
|---|---|---|
| **MaintainX** | Free tier; paid seats to ~$65; ~$39k+/yr for 50 Premium users; SSO, multi-site, IoT, report builder all gated to Enterprise tier ([Facilio pricing](https://facilio.com/blog/maintainx-pricing/), [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/maintainx/reviews)) | Asset-hierarchy depth, cross-site reporting, vendor/contract management, role-permission granularity, restricted customisation ([SoftwareAdvice](https://www.softwareadvice.com/cmms/maintainx-profile/), [Research.com](https://research.com/software/reviews/maintainx)) |
| **UpKeep** | $20–$75/user/mo published; key features (offline mobile, analytics) pushed to higher tiers ([Facilio comparison](https://facilio.com/blog/upkeep-vs-limble-cmms/)) | Slow UI/bugs reported; effective price higher than headline; lighter compliance tooling ([Limble comparison](https://limble.com/learn/upkeep-vs-fiix)) |
| **Limble** | ~$28–$69+/user/mo, custom-quoted ([ITQlick](https://www.itqlick.com/limble-cmms/pricing)) | Quote friction; 4–12 week implementations as hierarchy/integration depth grows ([Reliamag](https://reliamag.com/guides/limble-vs-upkeep/)) |
| **Fiix** (Rockwell) | $45–$75/user/mo ([Limble comparison](https://limble.com/learn/upkeep-vs-fiix)) | Manufacturing-asset orientation; weaker fit for buildings/estates and statutory-compliance workflows |
| **Fracttal** | Custom quotes; competitive entry, costs climb with IoT/analytics add-ons ([SelectHub](https://www.selecthub.com/p/cmms-software/fracttal/)) | LatAm-centred support/partner ecosystem; thin UK/Ireland presence ([CMMSAnalyst](https://www.cmms.app/vendors/fracttal)) |

### 3.3 The structural gap they leave

None of these vendors carries: statutory-compliance regimes (SFG20, fire/asbestos/legionella registers), public-sector audit trails, space/condition-survey models, FOI-defensible record-keeping, or any concept of a collection environment. Enterprise reviewers say so directly — multi-site portfolio managers cite asset hierarchy, cross-site reporting, and vendor management as MaintainX's gaps ([SoftwareAdvice](https://www.softwareadvice.com/cmms/maintainx-profile/)). They win the technician; they cannot pass a public-sector estates evaluation that scores statutory compliance and audit. **FMIQ's UX bar is MaintainX; its evaluation-criteria bar is Civica/MRI.**

## 4. Heritage / museum-specific FM tooling

### 4.1 What museums actually run

Three disconnected stacks:

1. **Generic CAFM bent to heritage.** English Heritage on Tribal K2 (438 sites; condition surveys, backlog, helpdesk, contractor portal) ([Tribal](https://www.tribalgroup.com/case-studies/english-heritage-chooses-tribals-k2-facilities-and-asset-management-system)); National Museums Liverpool managing 21 heritage buildings and 4M objects with mainstream FM tooling ([Comparesoft podcast](https://comparesoft.com/facilities-management-software/podcasts/managing-heritage-museum-buildings/)); universities (e.g. Reading) running CAFM/estates programmes ([Reading](https://www.reading.ac.uk/digital-technology-services/digital-strategy/digital-strategy-projects/cafm-and-estates-systems)). Niche players like Invida market specifically at buildings housing significant collections ([Invida](https://www.invida.co.uk/)).
2. **Collection-environment monitoring, separate from FM.** The conservation world runs its own stack: **Conserv** (LoRaWAN sensors + preservation analytics — TWPI, mould risk — hardware/software/support in one subscription, support staffed by conservators) ([Conserv](https://conserv.io/), [pricing](https://conserv.io/pricing/)); **Hanwell/IMC** loggers and EMS software (used at the Science Museum) ([Science Museum blog](https://blog.sciencemuseum.org.uk/environmental-monitoring-at-the-science-museum/)); **Eltek Darca Heritage** ([CAS Dataloggers](https://dataloggerinc.com/resource-article/environmental-monitoring-museums/)). Standards context: PAS 198/BS 4971-type environmental envelopes; 16–25°C and 40–60% RH bands with fluctuation limits ([NEDCC](https://www.nedcc.org/free-resources/preservation-leaflets/2.-the-environment/2.2-monitoring-temperature-and-relative-humidity)).
3. **Collections management systems** (PastPerfect, Lucidea Argus, Axiell) — object records, loans, conservation treatment. Not FM, but the registrar's system of record that FM data never reaches ([Lucidea Argus](https://lucidea.com/argus/)).

### 4.2 The gap FMIQ can own

No product on the market closes the loop between **the building and the collection**:

- An RH excursion in a gallery is a *conservation incident* and a *maintenance work order* — today those live in two systems owned by two departments (conservation vs estates), reconciled by email.
- Nobody maps environmental zones to FM assets (the AHU that serves Gallery 3) so that a preservation alert auto-raises a prioritised, SLA-tracked work order with the right contractor and the conservation context attached.
- Nobody gives a museum director one view: statutory compliance (fire, asbestos, legionella, BCAR), maintenance backlog by protected-structure constraint, and collection-environment performance — the exact triad the NMI PMC describes across four public sites plus stores.
- Conserv has the sensors and conservator credibility but no work-order, contractor, compliance, or estates layer; CAFM vendors have the estates layer but treat a gallery like an office floor.

FMIQ's ownable category: **heritage estate operations — FM + statutory compliance + collection-care environment in one system**, with Conserv-class sensor ingestion (integrate, don't build hardware) and CAFM-class work management.

### 4.3 Eligibility caution (NMI-specific)

The NMI PMC explicitly wants a proven, already-live, deployable solution and excludes offerings materially dependent on future development. A net-new FMIQ cannot honestly bid that tender at launch. The PMC's correct use is as a requirements blueprint and to shape evaluation criteria; the credible near-term routes are (a) lighthouse pilots at smaller heritage/cultural bodies where "established product" tests are lighter, (b) partnering with or wrapping a mature core, or (c) responding to the PMC to influence the future tender's evaluation model. This mirrors the strategic constraint already recorded in the project charter.

## 5. How Irish/UK public bodies buy IWMS

### 5.1 Routes to market

**Ireland.**
- All competitions run through **eTenders** ([etenders.gov.ie](https://www.etenders.gov.ie/)); OGP central frameworks cover ICT and facilities categories ([OGP](https://ogp.gov.ie/), [schedule of frameworks](https://ogp.gov.ie/schedule-of-frameworks-and-contracts/)).
- Practical thresholds: <€25k verbal quotes; €25–50k three written quotes; >€50k formal eTenders publication; EU-threshold (~€143k supplies/services for central bodies) triggers full EU procedure ([Duke guide](https://duke.yt/blog/how-to-find-government-contracts-ireland), [TenderRadar](https://tenderradar.io/guides/find-tenders-ireland)). A museum-scale IWMS contract will be above EU threshold → open procedure with published award criteria.
- **PMCs are now a formal eTenders notice type** (since August 2025) and increasingly precede system procurements ([gov.ie PMCs](https://www.gov.ie/en/office-of-government-procurement/publications/preliminary-market-consultations/)) — NMI's IWMS PMC and the National Library's "next generation library management system" PMC ([Ireland Tenders](https://www.irelandtenders.com/tender/preliminary-market-consultation-next-generation-national-library-management-system-7bf6792.php)) are the cultural-institution instances of this pattern. Sister-NCI procurements are a visible pipeline.
- Irish single-supplier CAFM precedent exists (TU Dublin single-party CAFM framework, via eTenders) ([search result](https://www.etenders.gov.ie/)); HSE Capital & Estates runs everything through eTenders ([Mercell/HSE](https://info.mercell.com/en-gb/meet-our-customers/hse-capital-estates/)).

**UK.**
- **G-Cloud (CCS)** is the fast route — pre-qualified SaaS catalogue, award in weeks; Planon, IBM, MRI, SWG, Micad all list ([Computer Weekly](https://www.computerweekly.com/feature/The-UK-governments-G-Cloud-procurement-framework-Everything-you-need-to-know), [SWG on G-Cloud](https://www.swg.com/news/using-g-cloud-for-easier-cafm-procurement/)). Getting FMIQ onto G-Cloud is a low-cost credibility and channel move.
- Above-threshold competitions publish on **Find a Tender** under the Procurement Act 2023, frequently preceded by preliminary market engagement notices — e.g. Oxford Health NHS FT's CAFM/IWMS procurement ([FTS 022586-2025](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/022586-2025)).

### 5.2 Contract values and shapes

- **Oxford Health NHS FT (CAFM + IWMS, 2025):** £800k ex-VAT over 5 years + 2 option years — i.e. roughly **£110–160k/year** for a multi-county NHS trust ([FTS 022586-2025](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/022586-2025)). This is the most concrete public benchmark and a realistic ceiling-shape for an NMI-scale contract.
- Typical term: 5 years with 1–2 extension years; CPV 48421000 (facilities management software package).
- Numerous 2025–2026 CAFM notices on FTS (Birmingham City University, NHS helpdesk/CAFM bundles, a 2026 CAFM procurement) show a steady deal flow in the £100k–£1M band ([FTS 013248-2025](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/013248-2025), [FTS 031869-2025](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/031869-2025), [FTS 012730-2026](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/012730-2026)).

### 5.3 Evaluation criteria observed

From recent CAFM tenders ([Birmingham City University notice](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/001904-2025), [FTS CAFM notices](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/013248-2025)):

- **Prior delivery evidence** — commensurate experience of similar-sized CAFM implementations, with named contract examples (the "already live" test in scoring form).
- **Financial standing** — credit-agency scores (e.g. Creditsafe 50%+) or equivalent demonstration.
- **Cyber/security certification** — Cyber Essentials/CE+ or ISO 27001 commonly mandatory. *FMIQ inherits a direct advantage here from the Nexum/GovIQ ISO 27001 programme.*
- **Quality:price weighting** with quality typically dominant for systems; demonstrations/presentations scored.
- **Implementation within timescale and continuity of service** as explicit requirements.
- Public-sector specifics that challenger CMMS vendors fail: data residency (EU/UK), FOI/audit defensibility, accessibility (WCAG), integration with finance systems.

**Implication.** The two hard gates for a young vendor are *reference deployments* and *security certification*. ISO 27001 (Nov 2026 target) covers the second; the first demands 2–3 paid lighthouse deployments — realistically smaller cultural/heritage bodies, Section 38/39-type organisations, local-authority museums — before bidding NCI-scale competitions.

## 6. FMIQ differentiation thesis

### 6.1 The market gap, stated precisely

| Capability | Enterprise IWMS (Planon/MRI/Civica) | Mobile CMMS (MaintainX class) | Conservation monitoring (Conserv class) | FMIQ target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Statutory compliance registers (fire/asbestos/legionella/BCAR) | Yes | No | No | Yes — Irish statutory model native |
| Mobile-first technician UX, hours-to-onboard | No | Yes | n/a | Yes |
| Collection-care environment (TWPI, RH bands, IPM) | No | No | Yes | Yes — fused with FM |
| Environmental alert → work order → contractor SLA loop | No | No | No | Yes — the signature workflow |
| Protected-structure / heritage asset semantics | No | No | Partial | Yes |
| Public-sector procurement fit (audit, FOI, residency, ISO 27001) | Yes | Weak | Weak | Yes — inherited from GovIQ ISMS |
| Self-serve onboarding / transparent pricing | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Deployment time | 9–18 months | Days–weeks | Days | Weeks, with guided estate import |

The whitespace is the column no one fills: **one system where the estate, the statutory record, and the collection environment are the same data model.**

### 6.2 Competitor weaknesses to exploit in UX

1. **Implementation time as the wedge.** Planon: 9–18 months, partner-mandatory ([Facilio](https://facilio.com/blog/planon-review/)). English Heritage's K2 project needed a bespoke validation/import utility just to load condition-survey spreadsheets ([Tribal](https://www.tribalgroup.com/case-studies/english-heritage-chooses-tribals-k2-facilities-and-asset-management-system)). FMIQ should make **estate onboarding a product feature**: spreadsheet/condition-survey import with validation holding-area as a self-serve flow, not a services engagement. Museums hold exactly this data shape (site → building → zone/gallery → asset, plus condition backlogs).
2. **Power-user UI as the foil.** Archibus reviewed as "too complex, created for power users", many steps per task ([TrustRadius](https://www.trustradius.com/products/archibus/reviews)); Planon's UI modernisation lags ([SelectHub](https://www.selecthub.com/p/iwms-software/planon/)). FMIQ's bar: any gallery attendant reports a defect with a photo in under 30 seconds; any conservator sees zone environment without training. Match MaintainX's 9.4/10 ease-of-use standard inside a compliance-grade system.
3. **Tier-gating resentment.** MaintainX locks SSO, multi-site, and report building behind Enterprise ([Facilio pricing](https://facilio.com/blog/maintainx-pricing/)). Public bodies read SSO-as-upsell as a security tax. FMIQ: SSO (Entra ID), audit trail, and statutory registers in every tier; price on estate size, not feature ransom.
4. **Quote-only pricing friction.** Every enterprise vendor is quote-based; UpKeep/Limble show transparent pricing accelerates evaluation ([Facilio comparison](https://facilio.com/blog/upkeep-vs-limble-cmms/)). Published, bands-by-estate-size pricing is itself differentiation in a public-sector evaluation, and aligns with OGP value-for-money scoring.
5. **The two-department email gap.** No competitor even attempts the conservation↔estates workflow (§4.2). This is the demo moment: sensor breach in a humidity-controlled store → auto-triaged work order with preservation-risk priority → contractor dispatched → closure evidence written to the statutory/audit record. Nobody else can show it.

### 6.3 Strategic posture vs the NMI constraint

- **Do not** position FMIQ as a bespoke NMI build; the PMC excludes it (§4.3).
- **Do** respond to the PMC to plant evaluation criteria the incumbents fail: collection-environment integration, onboarding time, transparent pricing, Irish data residency, ISO 27001.
- **Do** build the reference base bottom-up: county museums, OPW-adjacent heritage sites, Section 38/39 bodies with estates, university heritage buildings — contracts under or near EU threshold where "established product" tests are survivable, assembling the "already live" evidence the big tenders demand.
- **Watch** the sister-NCI pipeline (National Library LMS PMC is live — [Ireland Tenders](https://www.irelandtenders.com/tender/preliminary-market-consultation-next-generation-national-library-management-system-7bf6792.php)) and NHS/HSE estates digitalisation as the volume market behind the heritage wedge ([MRI NHS](https://www.mrisoftware.com/uk/healthcare-facilities-management-software/), [Micad NHS](https://www.micadsoftware.com/industry/nhs/)).
- **Reuse** GovIQ assets ruthlessly: ISO 27001 ISMS, audit-chain architecture, Entra ID auth, Irish public-sector procurement fluency — these are exactly the evaluation criteria in §5.3.

### 6.4 Risks to the thesis

- **MRI or Micad adds a "heritage pack."** Lowest-effort competitive response; mitigated by the conservation-domain depth (TWPI, IPM, preservation metrics) that requires credibility, not just fields.
- **Conserv moves up-stack into work orders.** They have conservator trust and sensor install base; watch their roadmap ([Conserv platform](https://conserv.io/environmental-monitoring-platform/)).
- **Procurement gravity.** Cultural institutions may bundle IWMS into wider managed-FM contracts where software is the contractor's choice — track FM outsourcing notices, not just software notices ([FTS FM provision examples](https://www.find-tender.service.gov.uk/Notice/076662-2025)).
- **Reference-base chicken-and-egg.** The §5.3 prior-delivery gate is the single biggest commercial risk; sequencing of lighthouse pilots is the mitigation and belongs in the execution plan.
